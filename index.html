Regional Context: A Shared Challenge

Insecurity is not an issue exclusive to Peru. When comparing victimization rates across the region, a complex panorama emerges that demands proactive security measures. Countries with similar economies and challenges, such as Mexico or Brazil, have already normalized the use of specialized transport to mitigate risks.

The Threat in Peru: Data and Trends

Cargo transport theft in Peru shows an alarming upward trend, with a constant increase in the frequency and violence of attacks. This results not only in direct economic losses but also in operational disruptions and risks to personnel.

Critical Risk Zones in Metropolitan Lima

Certain areas, especially entry and exit routes to the capital, concentrate the majority of incidents. Callao, as the main port and gateway to the country, is the epicenter of criminal activity, but the risk extends along the main road axes.

Callao: Epicenter of Risk

As the country's main port, it is the point of greatest vulnerability. Organized gangs operate in the vicinity of the port and airport, intercepting high-value cargo. Avenues Néstor Gambetta, Argentina, and Faucett are considered of maximum danger due to the confluence of trucks and ease of escape.

San Juan de Lurigancho: Complexity and Escape

Due to its population density and complex network of streets, it is a preferred area for criminals to 'twin' trucks and quickly hide stolen merchandise. Routes connecting to the Central Highway are critical points.

Ate Vitarte: Industrial Corridor

Being a key industrial corridor and the main access route to the Central Highway, it presents a high risk. Assaults often occur during high-traffic hours to hinder police response. The interception method using smaller vehicles is common.

San Martín de Porres: Northern Connection

This district is a key logistical hub for routes heading north of the country via the North Pan-American Highway. Gangs take advantage of informal stops and rest areas for trucks to plan and execute assaults, often with the complicity of informants.

Case Analysis: The Modus Operandi

The study of emblematic cases reveals clear patterns: detailed planning, use of technology for tracking (GPS jammers), violence, and a rapid distribution chain for stolen goods. These are not impulsive acts but structured criminal operations.

Case: "The Electronics of Callao"

Merchandise: High-end smartphones and laptops.

Estimated Loss: $1.2 Million USD.

A truck was intercepted at the port exit by four vehicles. They used GPS signal blockers to prevent tracking and moved the cargo to a clandestine warehouse in less than 25 minutes. The driver was held and released hours later.

Case: "The Highway Twinning"

Merchandise: Telecommunications equipment.

Estimated Loss: $850,000 USD.

On the Central Highway, a fake police operation stopped the truck. Within minutes, an identical 'twin' vehicle replaced it on the route with false documentation, while the real cargo was diverted. The company took 6 hours to detect the anomaly.

Case: "The Pharmaceutical Robbery"

Merchandise: A batch of high-cost medicines.

Estimated Loss: $2.1 Million USD.

The criminal gang had inside information about the route and the value of the cargo. The assault occurred during an unscheduled stop by the driver. The merchandise was quickly introduced into the black market, demonstrating a sophisticated distribution network.
